# story-structures

## Purpose

A body without an arc rambles. Pick one structure, outline the beats, then write. The structure is the skeleton. The voice is the skin.

## The structures

### Problem, shift, proof
1. Name the problem the reader feels.
2. Shift to the truth they have not considered.
3. Prove it with a specific number, name, or result.
Use when teaching (the Education pillar).

### Before and after
1. Show the before state honestly.
2. Show what changed and the one thing that drove it.
3. Show the after, with proof.
Use for transformation and results (the Proof pillar).

### Myth correction
1. State the myth the audience believes.
2. Show why it is wrong, plainly.
3. Replace it with the simpler truth and what to do instead.
Use to debunk hype (the Education pillar).

### List with a turn
1. Open with a tight, specific list.
2. Walk the items fast.
3. Turn at the end: the one that actually mattered, or the one to skip.
Use for practical, scannable posts.

### Behind the curtain
1. Open on a real moment from the work.
2. Show the decision or mistake inside it.
3. Land on what it taught, in one line.
Use for founder and process posts (the Behind the studio pillar).

## How to use

1. Read the locked pillar, angle, and hook from content-os.
2. Pick the arc that fits the pillar and the hook's promise.
3. Outline three or four beats in one line each. Lock the outline.
4. Write the body to the outline. One idea only. End on the turn, not a recap.

## Self-test

- Does each beat earn the next?
- Is there exactly one idea, or did a second sneak in?
- Does the ending land a point, or just stop?

## Rules

- One idea per post.
- No em dashes or hyphens as breaks. No emojis.
- Show, do not claim. Every claim needs a number, a name, or a result.
