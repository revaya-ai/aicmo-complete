# publish-linkedin

## Purpose

A post can be on brand and still die in the LinkedIn feed because of formatting. This skill is the format layer: how the words sit on the screen so the first line earns the click and the body stays readable. It changes spacing and structure, never the message.

## The first line is the whole game

LinkedIn truncates after roughly two short lines with a "see more". The hook has to open a gap that makes the reader tap.
- Lead with the hook, alone, on line one.
- Keep line one under about 10 words.
- No throat-clearing. No "I wanted to share". No date stamps.
- Never put the payoff in line one. Open the loop, do not close it.

## Line breaks and rhythm

- One idea per line cluster. Break often.
- Use single blank lines between thoughts. White space pulls the eye down.
- Keep paragraphs to one to three lines. A wall of text gets scrolled past.
- Short line, short line, then a longer one. Vary the rhythm so it does not drone.

## Length

- Aim for 900 to 1,300 characters for a standard post.
- Long enough to say one thing well. Short enough to finish in the feed without a second "see more".

## Hashtags

- Three to five, maximum. All at the very bottom, after the body.
- No hashtag walls. No hashtags mid-sentence.
- Use specific tags the audience actually follows, not generic ones like #motivation.

## Links

- Put any link in the first comment, not the body. A link in the body suppresses reach.
- If a link must go in the body, place it at the very end.

## Banned in the feed

- No emoji bullet lists pretending to be design.
- No "Agree?" or "Thoughts?" engagement bait at the end. No CTA at all unless the brief asks for one, and then only a direct outreach action.
- No em dashes or hyphens as sentence breaks. Use commas or periods.
- No "in today's fast-paced world" style openers.

## The check before you ship

Read line one alone. If it does not make you want to tap "see more", rewrite line one. Then read the whole thing top to bottom and confirm: one idea, native spacing, hashtags at the bottom, no engagement bait.

## What this skill does NOT do

It does not rewrite the idea, the angle, or the voice. Those are locked upstream by content-os and writing-style. This is the last formatting pass before the post goes to Zernio.
