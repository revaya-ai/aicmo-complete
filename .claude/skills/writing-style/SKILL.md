# writing-style

## Purpose

Make every post read like a sharp human wrote it, not a model. The content-os Brick chain loads this skill at the Story/Shift step. The body is not locked until it passes the self-check at the bottom.

## Hard rules

- No em dashes or hyphens used as sentence breaks. Use commas or periods.
- No emojis.
- Show, do not claim. Every claim carries a number, a name, or a date, or it gets cut. Never invent a statistic.
- One idea per post. If a second idea shows up, save it for another post.
- Write for one reader. Use "you" and, where the brand is two founders, "we".
- Read the client voice file first. The brand voice always wins over these defaults.

## Banned phrases

Cut these on sight. They are the fingerprints of generic AI copy:

- in today's fast-paced world
- it's important to note
- at the end of the day
- when it comes to
- unlock (as in "unlock your potential")
- leverage
- elevate / elevate your routine
- game-changer
- revolutionary / revolutionize
- dive in / let's dive in / deep dive
- delve
- in conclusion / in summary
- the world of (as in "the world of skincare")
- look no further
- tried and true
- nestled
- testament to
- navigate the landscape
- supercharge
- harness the power of

## Sentence craft

- Mix short and long sentences on purpose. Never three sentences in a row with the same shape.
- A one-sentence paragraph is allowed for emphasis. Use it rarely so it keeps its punch.
- Start sentences with "So", "And", "But", "Because" when it sounds natural.
- Cut filler openers. Get to the useful thing in the first line.
- Prefer plain words. If a simpler word works, use it.

## Self-check (run before the body is locked)

Answer each. If any answer is "no", fix the body and re-check.

1. Zero em dashes or hyphens used as breaks?
2. Zero banned phrases?
3. Every claim backed by a specific, or cut?
4. One idea, not two?
5. First line earns the read, no throat-clearing?
6. Sentence lengths vary, no robotic rhythm?
7. Reads in the client's voice, not a generic brand voice?
